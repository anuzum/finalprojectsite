Food as Cultural Identity

Georgia, a small country in Eastern Europe roughly the size of West Virginia, holds deep roots in its traditions of hospitality. Georgian cooking is full of abundant, colorful, delightful dishes that are rich in fresh vegetables, herbs, spices, and flavors.

Georgian Supra

A Georgian supra, or a traditional feast, is usually a long table filled with an abundance of dishes, from local vegetables to skewered meats and stews. Your plate is never empty for long.

Georgian Tamada

The supra is led by a tamada, or toastmaster, who toasts throughout the meal. Georgian toasts are unique, meaningful, and always punctuated by "gaumarjos!" which means victory.

Georgian Wine

Georgia is the birthplace of winemaking, producing wine as early as 6,000 B.C. The ancient Georgian method of qvevri winemaking is designated by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. Georgia's wine and winemaking roots are deeply embedded into its traditions of hospitality and cuisine.
